# mirraw.com — Research Document

## Overview
- **URL**: https://www.mirraw.com
- **Platform**: Custom-built marketplace (not Shopify — multi-seller, multi-vendor architecture)
- **Segment**: Multi-designer marketplace for Indian ethnic wear, jewellery, accessories, home decor
- **Target Audience**: Women aged 22–50, fashion-conscious, value seekers, India + NRI
- **Brand Vibe**: "Celebrating Indian Art & Fashion" — designer marketplace, wide selection

## Selling Strategy
- **Marketplace Model**: Largest selection (5000+ designers) — infinite inventory without holding stock
- **Deep Discounting**: Heavily discount-driven (40–80% off) — price-sensitive audience
- **First Order Discount**: 10% off for newsletter signup
- **Free Shipping**: Low threshold (₹499) — almost everyone qualifies
- **COD**: Available — critical for first-time online shoppers
- **Returns**: 7-day return policy
- **Curated Shopping**: "Shop by Occasion", "Shop by Price", "Wedding Store" guide discovery
- **Designer Discovery**: Designer store fronts + editorial picks build designer brand awareness
- **Content Marketing**: Blog with fashion trends, styling tips, wedding guides
- **Social Proof**: Reviews, ratings, "Trending Now", "Best Seller" tags
- **Email/SMS**: Abandoned cart, browse abandonment, back-in-stock, price drop (standard marketplace features)
- **Urgency**: Low stock indicators, limited time sale timers
- **Commission Model**: Takes cut from designer sales — scalable without inventory risk

## Strengths
- Massive selection (5000+ designers) — unbeatable variety
- Heavy discounts attract price-sensitive shoppers
- Marketplace model scales without inventory investment
- Rich filtering + sorting makes large catalog navigable
- Strong trust signals (COD, easy returns, free shipping, reviews)
- Multi-brand discoverability (shop by designer)
- Excellent SEO (millions of product pages indexed)
- Established brand (one of oldest Indian fashion marketplaces)
- Mobile app + responsive web
- Wedding store microsite captures high-intent bridal traffic

## Weaknesses / Gaps
- Product quality / consistency varies by designer (marketplace risk)
- Design is functional but not premium (cluttered compared to D2C brands)
- Shipping times vary by designer (not always controlled)
- Returns process can be complex (multi-vendor)
- Customer service is marketplace level (less personal than D2C)
- Heavy discount positioning devalues brand perception
- Not all designers offer blouse stitching
- Product imagery quality varies by designer

## Unique Differentiators
- Largest multi-designer marketplace model among competitors
- 5000+ designers under one roof
- Wedding store microsite with curated bridal collections
- Price drop + back-in-stock alerts
- Seller/designer registration platform
- Bulk/corporate orders page
- Store locator for franchise pickup points
