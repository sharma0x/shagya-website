# Saree E-Commerce — Selling Strategy Analysis

## Common Patterns Across All 7 Sites

### 1. Discount-Driven Conversion
- **Every site** displays a discount percentage (strike-through MRP vs selling price)
- Discounts range from 20% to 80% off
- **mirraw** leads with the highest discounts (40–80% off), leveraging marketplace competition
- **suta** is the exception — minimal discounting, relies on brand value and premium pricing
- Seasonal sales amplify discounts (Diwali, Pongal, Wedding Season, End of Season)

### 2. Free Shipping as Expected Default
- 3/7 offer free shipping on **all orders** (houseofraadhvi, karagiri, pothys)
- 3/7 offer free shipping above a low threshold (utsavFashion ₹X, nalli ₹999, mirraw ₹499)
- **suta** offers free shipping on prepaid orders only — incentivizes digital payments
- Free shipping is now table stakes; not offering it is a conversion killer

### 3. First-Order Discount for Email Capture
- **utsavFashion**: 10% off
- **nalli**: Flat 5% off
- **suta**: 10% off
- **mirraw**: 10% off
- **houseofraadhvi, karagiri, pothys**: No first-order discount observed
- Those who don't offer it miss a key conversion lever

### 4. COD as Trust Backstop
- All sites that serve the Indian market offer COD (except suta — prepaid focus)
- COD is critical for first-time online saree buyers in India
- Some restrict COD above a certain order value (mirraw, pothys)

### 5. Easy Returns as Purchase Risk Reducer
- **utsavFashion**: Easy returns (14-day)
- **nalli**: 14-day return policy
- **karagiri**: Easy returns
- **mirraw**: 7-day return policy
- **pothys**: Easy returns
- **houseofraadhvi**: Returns policy present (not prominently highlighted)
- **suta**: Returns policy present but not prominently featured
- 14 days is standard; anything less (7 days, mirraw) is below market expectation

## Business Model Strategies

### D2C Brands (houseofraadhvi, suta, karagiri)
- **Control**: Full control over product, pricing, branding, customer experience
- **Margins**: Higher margins (no marketplace commission)
- **Inventory Risk**: Carry inventory — need to manage stock, markdowns
- **Customer Ownership**: Direct access to customer data, email, SMS
- **Marketing**: Invest in brand building, content, social media, influencer marketing
- **Key Focus**: Brand differentiation, customer experience, repeat purchases
- **Risk**: Scaling catalog requires upfront investment

### Marketplace (mirraw)
- **Scale**: Infinite catalog without inventory — 5000+ designers
- **Margins**: Commission-based (lower per-order margin but zero inventory risk)
- **Selection**: Unbeatable variety — wins on breadth
- **Customer Attention**: Aggregation marketplace — customers come for selection, not brand
- **Pricing**: Heavily discount-driven as designers compete on price
- **Quality Risk**: Inconsistent quality across sellers
- **Key Focus**: Seller acquisition, review quality, shipping reliability, search/discovery
- **Risk**: Customer service complexity (multi-vendor), brand dilution

### Retail + Online (nalli, pothys, utsavFashion)
- **Trust Factor**: Decades of physical retail establish immediate online trust
- **Omnichannel**: Physical stores for browse+pickup, online for convenience+reach
- **Inventory**: Deep catalog from store inventory + warehouse
- **Customer Base**: Built-in customer base from physical retail
- **Key Focus**: Digital integration, click & collect, inventory unification
- **Risk**: Legacy tech, slower digital innovation, cannibalization fears

## Pricing Strategies

| Strategy | Sites Using | Description |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| **High-Low Pricing** | houseofraadhvi, mirraw, nalli, pothys, karagiri | List high MRP, sell at deep discount — creates perceived value |
| **Premium / Value-Based** | suta | Price based on brand + craftsmanship, minimal discounts |
| **Competitive Pricing** | karagiri | Price competitively with moderate discounts (20-50%) |
| **Marketplace Dynamic** | mirraw | Designers set own prices, platform drives discount competition |
| **Free Shipping Loss Leader** | karagiri, pothys | Absorb shipping cost, drive higher AOV to compensate |

## Conversion Optimization Tactics

### What's Working (Observed on Multiple Sites)
- ✅ Product variant swatches on listing pages (all 7)
- ✅ Discount % badges prominent (all 7)
- ✅ Free shipping banner in utility bar (6/7)
- ✅ COD availability display (5/7)
- ✅ Easy returns policy mention (5/7)
- ✅ Related products cross-sell on PDP (all 7)
- ✅ Wishlist save for later (all 7)
- ✅ Newsletter signup (all 7)
- ✅ Social proof: Best Seller badges (karagiri, pothys, mirraw)

### What's Underutilized (Missed Opportunities)
- ❌ **Urgency** — Only utsavFashion and mirraw use low-stock/timer tactics
- ❌ **Abandoned Cart** — Likely only mirraw and utsavFashion have robust flows
- ❌ **Price Drop Alerts** — Only mirraw has this
- ❌ **Back-in-Stock Alerts** — Only utsavFashion and mirraw
- ❌ **First-Order Discount** — 3/7 don't use this
- ❌ **Video Drape Guides** — Dramatically underutilized (saree education gap)
- ❌ **Live Chat** — Only utsavFashion and mirraw have it
- ❌ **Loyalty Program** — Only utsavFashion has rewards
- ❌ **Combo Bundles** — Only karagiri actively bundles saree+blouse

## Key Takeaways for a New Saree E-Commerce Site

1. **Minimum Viable Feature Set**: Variants, search, filters, wishlist, free shipping, COD, easy returns, newsletter + first-order discount, discount badges, related products
2. **Differentiator Opportunities**: Video drape guides, blouse stitching service, combo bundles, loyalty program, live chat, abandoned cart emails, blog/content
3. **Avoid**: Market race-to-bottom discounting (mirraw model). Instead build brand value (suta model).
4. **Must-Have Trust Signals**: SSL badge, return policy, shipping info, contact info, about us, social proof
5. **International Consideration**: Currency converter, zone-specific shipping, PayPal — if targeting NRIs
6. **Content is the Moat**: suta's blog + Instagram community is their strongest competitive advantage
7. **Mobile-First**: app optional but responsive design mandatory — 70%+ of traffic is mobile
