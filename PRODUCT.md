# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Indian women, 28–50, buying sarees for themselves. She knows her weaves — she can tell a Banarasi from a Chanderi — and arrives on the homepage looking for a trustworthy source that takes the craft as seriously as she does. She is not browsing casually; she has intention. She wants to be shown, not sold to.

## Product Purpose

Shagya is a direct-to-consumer handloom saree marketplace. It connects buyers to weaving clusters across India — maker-traced, no middlemen — and sells silk, cotton, and heritage weaves sourced directly from artisans. Success looks like a customer who trusts the provenance, understands the craft, and returns because the experience matched the quality.

## Brand Personality

Heritage · Quiet · Trusted. The brand speaks with the confidence of a specialist, not the enthusiasm of a salesperson. Understated and authoritative. Rooted in craft knowledge, not trend. The voice is warm but precise — like a letter from someone who knows exactly what they're talking about.

## Anti-references

Not Sabyasachi-editorial: too dark, too inaccessible, too focused on spectacle over sale. The customer should be able to browse and buy, not merely admire. Not mass-market Indian e-commerce (Myntra/Meesho): no discount banners, no busy tile grids, no pressure. Not generic-minimalist Western luxury: too cold, too European, too far from the cultural context.

## Design Principles

1. **Show, don't announce.** The craft earns trust through specificity — named weaves, named clusters, maker data — not through superlatives or marketing language. Section headings lead; they don't need a label above them announcing what they are.
2. **Quiet confidence.** Every design decision defaults to restraint. If an element can be removed and the page still communicates clearly, remove it.
3. **Respects her expertise.** The user knows sarees. Copy and presentation assume this. No over-explaining; no condescension.
4. **Maker before market.** The artisan story is the brand differentiator. Layout and hierarchy should surface the human behind the cloth, not just the product and price.
5. **Completeness over decoration.** A polished empty state, a graceful error, a clear loading signal — these signal professionalism more than decorative elements do.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. All text meets 4.5:1 for small text, 3:1 for large. Reduced motion respected for all animations. Keyboard navigation complete. Screen-reader labels on all interactive elements. Devanagari script support via Noto Sans Devanagari.
